Problem Statement

Traditional voting systems face issues of centralization, tampering risks, manual result delays, and duplicate voting. Trust depends on authorities rather than verifiable proof. A decentralized mechanism is required where each vote is independently verifiable and permanently recorded.

Literature Review / Market Research

Research from IEEE and ACM highlights blockchain’s ability to provide transparent ledgers and tamper resistance. Estonia’s national e-voting demonstrated digital adoption but relied on centralized verification. Projects like Voatz and FollowMyVote explored blockchain but required complex smart contracts and backend infrastructure. Most academic solutions prioritize security but lack accessibility for educational and small-scale environments.

Research Gap / Innovation

Our system focuses on lightweight simulation demonstrating core blockchain principles — hashing, chaining, validation, and tamper detection — without heavy infrastructure. It emphasizes clarity, educational usability, and interactive visualization of blockchain integrity.
